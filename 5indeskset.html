5" deskset

Product Line Renewal

Category Product design
Brand Language
My Role Product Designer
Duration 2 weeks
May - June 2019



01

Overview

Snom is a German company which manufactures Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) telephones, and was acquired by VTech Telecommunications in 2016. I was in the in-house design team responsible for the designs of Snom products.

This was an in-house design project to renew the Snom deskset series, which was expected to release a 5-inch model at the first stage. Working closely with the product manager, I was required to complete the design, which was then presented at the internal industrial design meeting in two weeks' time.


Challenge

Inheriting the signature brand language of Snom and adopting the latest design trend

Building a striking design which adapts the ambience of modern workplace


Solution

Implementing the look and feel, keypad layout and icons of the old Snom models

Applying new forms and colour combinations of office products to the new design



02

Design Trend Research

Design Language of Snom

At first, I started with a brainstorming session to think about the design languages of Snom. Reviewing the previous series, I came up with two signature points - the modest look and feel, as well as the legend keypad layout and icons.

Moodboard

After defining the signature design language of Snom, I started to look for some references in terms of modern workplace products, subtle form transformation and material finishing, which might be the inspirations for ideation.

Three images from each of the categories were then chosen as references for the ideation stage.


Modern Workplace


Form Transformation


Material Finishing



03

Ideation

The ideation process lasted for four days. I had meetings with the product manager every other day in order to present my ideas and sketches based on the references, as well as receiving comments from the manager so that the design would align with the Snom design language.

The idea of integrating two components with layered effect was then chosen by the product manager to further develop into a more refined concpet.



04

Final Design

The 3D cad model and the rendering of the final design were completed in the second week. Some manufacturing issues had to be considered during this process, ensuring the front cabinet could be made using only two moulds and minimising the cost of manufacture. Some minor modifications were done including adjusting the spacing of the key layout, as well as the shapes of keys. The outlook of the design was then finalised by applying desirable material finishing, ensuring the expected look and feel were shown on the rendering.


Keys and Interaction

Black key colour differentiates itself from the body.
The spacing and grouping of keys also facilitates intuitive interaction.


Look and Feel

The combination of black and space grey associates with modest and professional.
